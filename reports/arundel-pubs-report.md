# Pubs in Arundel, West Sussex: A Comprehensive Report

*Compiled February 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

Arundel is a compact Sussex market town with a pub scene that punches well above its weight. Approximately 12 venues currently trade within the town itself, with another 4-5 notable outlets in satellite villages (Burpham, Binsted, Offham, Houghton). The landscape is shaped by three dominant forces: the **Duke of Norfolk's estate** (which historically owned or leased much of the town's hospitality), **Hall & Woodhouse (Badger Breweries)** (operating multiple tied houses), and a growing **independent/craft brewing** movement led by Arundel Brewery and Fauna Brewing. Fuller's holds a single high-profile asset in The Swan Hotel. Harvey's of Lewes holds the White Hart as its sole Arun-district outpost. The food-led gastropub trend is strong — The George at Burpham, community-owned since 2006, ranks in the UK's Top 50 Gastropubs — while traditional wet-led locals like the Kings Arms (est. 1625) endure alongside modern craft taprooms. At least 21 pubs have closed over the centuries, reflecting both historical contraction and the shift toward fewer, higher-quality venues. The overall trend is toward premiumisation, local provenance, accommodation-led models, and conservation/mission-driven brewing.

## 1. Currently Trading Pubs in Arundel Town

### 1.1 The Kings Arms
- **Address:** 36 Tarrant Street, BN18 9DN
- **Established:** c. 1625
- **Ownership:** Family-run independent; free house
- **Listed status:** Grade II
- **Positioning:** Traditional wet-led drinkers' pub. No food served (BYO takeaway policy). Two bars, patio garden, board games, real ales on handpump.
- **Notable:** Arundel's oldest pub. A CAMRA-listed community local that has deliberately resisted the gastropub trend.
- **Sources:** [WhatPub](https://whatpub.com/pubs/AAA/10/kings-arms-arundel), [Pubs Galore](https://www.pubsgalore.co.uk/pubs/39346/), [CAMRA](https://camra.org.uk/pubs/112366)

### 1.2 The Eagle Inn
- **Address:** 41 Tarrant Street / corner of Brewery Hill, BN18 9DJ
- **Established:** Historic (former Eagle Brewery tap)
- **Ownership:** Independent free house
- **Positioning:** Traditional pub, recently transitioned to wet-led only (stopped serving food late 2024). Live music at weekends, log fire, large-screen sports.
- **Notable:** Sits on the site of the former Eagle Brewery (operated 1780-1935 by the Puttock and Norris families). The brewery was once a major employer and pub owner in the area before being absorbed by Friary Brewery and eventually liquidated.
- **Sources:** [CAMRA](https://camra.org.uk/pubs/eagle-arundel-112705), [Brewery History Society](https://breweryhistory.com/wiki/index.php/Eagle_Brewery,_Arundel)

### 1.3 The Red Lion
- **Address:** 45 High Street, BN18 9AG
- **Established:** Over 300 years old
- **Ownership:** Independent
- **Positioning:** Traditional pub with live music programme (Jailhouse Blues Club, open mic nights). Food-and-drink balanced.
- **Notable:** Prominent red-brick building on the main street. One of the most visible pubs in the town centre.
- **Sources:** [redlionarundel.com](https://www.redlionarundel.com/), [VisitArundel](https://www.visitarundel.co.uk/listing/the-red-lion/)

### 1.4 The Swan Hotel
- **Address:** High Street (facing Town Quay)
- **Established:** 1759
- **Ownership:** Fuller, Smith & Turner (Fuller's)
- **Listed status:** Grade II (Victorian)
- **Positioning:** Hotel-pub with 14 ensuite rooms, restaurant, and Fuller's ale bar. Refined pub classics, seasonal menus, pub quizzes, tribute nights. Visit Britain Breakfast Award winner.
- **Notable:** The only Fuller's house in Arundel. 200-year-old wine cellar. Refurbished 2004 and 2012. Rooms themed after Sussex rivers and South Downs landmarks.
- **Sources:** [swanarundel.co.uk](https://www.swanarundel.co.uk/), [Fuller's](https://www.swanarundel.co.uk/history)

### 1.5 The White Swan
- **Address:** Chichester Road (A27), BN18 0AD
- **Established:** 1690
- **Ownership:** Independent (ownership not publicly disclosed)
- **Positioning:** 4-star country inn with 20 rooms. Flagstone floors, oak beams, wood-burning stove, afternoon teas. Food-and-accommodation led.
- **Notable:** Separate venue from The Swan Hotel. Traditional country inn feel on the edge of town. Wedding and events venue.
- **Sources:** [white-swan-arundel.co.uk](https://white-swan-arundel.co.uk/)

### 1.6 St Mary's Gate Inn
- **Address:** London Road / King Street, BN18 9BA
- **Established:** 16th century (c. 1500s)
- **Ownership:** Hall & Woodhouse (Badger Breweries) — tied house
- **First licensed publican:** Henry Mackett, March 1764 (described as a "Tapster")
- **Positioning:** Historic pub-hotel with 6-7 ensuite rooms. Dining room, rear terrace, views toward Arundel Cathedral. Serves Badger ales alongside guest beers.
- **Historical ownership:** Building originally owned by tradesmen including a stonemason, linen draper, and wine merchant before becoming licensed premises.
- **Notable:** One of the town's most architecturally significant pubs. Boutique hotel operation alongside traditional pub.
- **Sources:** [stmarysgate.co.uk](https://stmarysgate.co.uk/about-us/), [Hall & Woodhouse](https://www.hall-woodhouse.co.uk/pub/st-marys-gate/), [CAMRA](https://camra.org.uk/pubs/st-marys-gate-inn-arundel-112398)

### 1.7 Norfolk Arms Hotel / The Norfolk Tap
- **Address:** 22 High Street, BN18 9AB
- **Established:** Early 1800s (Georgian coaching inn)
- **Original builder:** The 10th Duke of Norfolk
- **Current operator:** The Norfolk Arms Hotel Ltd (Company No. 08313852)
- **Listed status:** Grade II*
- **Positioning:** The hotel houses two distinct hospitality outlets:
  - **Norfolk Arms Hotel** — 4-star, 37 ensuite rooms, Arun Restaurant, events/conference venue
  - **The Norfolk Tap** — Modern craft beer bar and coffee shop showcasing Arundel Brewery and Sussex craft beers. New York industrial aesthetic.
- **Notable:** The Arundel Society for the Prosecution of Thieves and Felons still holds its annual dinner here, a tradition dating to the 18th century. TripAdvisor Travellers' Choice 2024. New GM (Karl Lever) appointed July 2024.
- **Sources:** [norfolkarmsarundel.com](https://www.norfolkarmsarundel.com/), [norfolktap.com](https://norfolktap.com/), [Historic England](https://historicengland.org.uk/listing/the-list/list-entry/1027908)

### 1.8 The White Hart
- **Address:** Queen Street, BN18 9JG
- **Established:** Historic
- **Ownership:** Harvey's Brewery of Lewes — tied house
- **Positioning:** Traditional pub with log fire, scrubbed wooden tables, old brewery posters. 4 handpumps serving Harvey's Sussex Best Bitter and seasonal ales. Basic rooms available. Courtyard drinking area.
- **Notable:** The only pub on the east bank of the River Arun. The sole Harvey's tied house in the entire Arun & Adur CAMRA branch area (acquired by Harvey's c. 2013).
- **Sources:** [Harvey's Brewery](https://www.harveys.org.uk/pub/white-hart-arundel), [CAMRA](https://camra.org.uk/pubs/white-hart-arundel-112420), [WhatPub](https://whatpub.com/pubs/AAA/15/white-hart-arundel)

## 2. Brewery Taprooms

### 2.1 Arundel Brewery Taphouse (The Brewhouse Project)
- **Location:** Just off the A27, near Arundel
- **Established:** Brewery founded 1992; taproom opened 2019
- **Ownership:** Family-owned independent (Stuart and family took over in 2015)
- **Positioning:** Modern taproom with craft beer, specialty coffee, street food. Capacity ~200. Plans for visible on-site brewing.
- **Hours:** Friday 4-8pm, Saturday-Sunday 10am-4pm
- **Notable:** Arundel Brewery is the town's original modern craft brewery. Beers also featured at The Norfolk Tap.
- **Sources:** [arundelbrewery.co.uk](https://www.arundelbrewery.co.uk/pages/arundel-taphouse)

### 2.2 Fauna Brewing Taproom
- **Address:** The Old Engine Shed, 6a London Road, BN18 9AT
- **Established:** Brewery founded 2020 by Philip Fitzalan Howard; Arundel taproom opened c. 2023
- **Ownership:** Fauna Brewing (founded by the youngest son of the Duke and Duchess of Norfolk)
- **Positioning:** Conservation-focused craft brewery taproom. 60+ seated inside, 200+ total capacity. Beer garden, wildflower meadow. Every beer supports a wildlife charity.
- **Notable:** The building is the Victorian Old Engine Shed that originally housed Arundel Castle's electricity generator. Formerly Dunlop Stables and Norfolk Estate storage. Phil Fitzalan Howard studied zoology and trained as a bush ranger in Botswana before founding the brewery. Partners include Cheetah Conservation Fund, Painted Dog Conservation, African Pangolin Working Group.
- **Sources:** [faunabrewing.com](https://www.faunabrewing.com/arundel-taproom), [Sussex Exclusive](https://sussexexclusive.com/what-do-fauna-brewing-arundel-castle-cheetahs-have-in-common/)

## 3. Pubs in Satellite Villages (within 3 miles)

### 3.1 The Black Rabbit
- **Address:** Mill Road, Offham, BN18 9PB
- **Ownership:** Duke of Norfolk estate (freehold); operated by Hall & Woodhouse (Badger Breweries)
- **Positioning:** Destination riverside pub-restaurant. Large outdoor terrace with views of Arundel Castle and the River Arun. Badger ales, Sunday roasts, seasonal menus. Dog-friendly.
- **Notable:** One of England's most photographed pubs. Harry Potter memorabilia (scenes filmed at nearby Arundel Cathedral). Adjacent to the WWT Arundel Wetland Centre. Classic day-tripper destination.
- **Sources:** [theblackrabbitarundel.co.uk](https://www.theblackrabbitarundel.co.uk/), [CAMRA](https://camra.org.uk/pubs/black-rabbit-inn-arundel-112683)

### 3.2 The George at Burpham
- **Address:** Main Street, Burpham (3 miles from Arundel)
- **Established:** c. 1736 (as the George & Dragon)
- **Ownership:** Community-owned since 2006 by Burpham & Warningcamp Pub Company Ltd (16 shareholders)
- **Listed status:** Grade II
- **Current management:** Martin & Charlotte Bull (since 2023)
- **Positioning:** Award-winning gastropub. Modern British seasonal menu using local ingredients (20-mile sourcing radius). Single bar serving Harvey's Sussex Best Bitter and Fauna ales.
- **Notable:** Rescued from closure by local residents. No. 72 in Top 50 Gastropubs 2025. No. 35 in Good Food Guide UK Top 100 Pubs 2025. AA Rosette. Sussex Life "Community Pub of the Year 2015".
- **Sources:** [georgeatburpham.co.uk](https://www.georgeatburpham.co.uk/), [CAMRA](https://camra.org.uk/pubs/george-at-burpham-burpham-112472), [Top 50 Gastropubs](https://www.top50gastropubs.com/Pubs/UK/Surrey/The-George.html)

### 3.3 The Black Horse
- **Address:** Binsted Lane, Binsted, BN18 0LP (off the A27)
- **Ownership:** Clive & Victoria Smith — family-run independent (The Black Horse Inn (Binsted) Limited, Company No. 02889838, incorporated 1994)
- **Positioning:** Smart food-led pub with conservatory/garden room. Views over Binsted golf course and countryside. Highly rated cooking.
- **Notable:** One of the most consistently well-reviewed dining pubs in the Arundel orbit.
- **Sources:** [theblackhorsebinsted.com](https://theblackhorsebinsted.com/), [Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/02889838)

### 3.4 The George & Dragon, Houghton
- **Address:** Houghton (3 miles north of Arundel)
- **Ownership:** Independent
- **Positioning:** Country pub on the South Downs Way. Walking/cycling trade alongside local regulars.
- **Sources:** [thegeorgeanddragonhoughton.co.uk](http://www.thegeorgeanddragonhoughton.co.uk/)

### 3.5 The Welldiggers Arms
- **Address:** Petworth Road, Low Heath (5 miles north-east)
- **Ownership:** Butcombe (regional pub company)
- **Positioning:** Boutique inn with 14 ensuite rooms. Recently refurbished and reopened November 2025. Country pub-restaurant.
- **Sources:** [butcombe.com](https://butcombe.com/the-welldiggers-arms-west-sussex/)

## 6. The Eagle Brewery Legacy

The Eagle Brewery on Brewery Hill was Arundel's most significant brewing operation:

- **1780:** Edward Puttock purchased the brewhouse leasehold
- **Early 1800s-1840:** George & Edward Bowden Puttock operated with Thomas Constable Junior
- **1872:** Properties taken over by H. Harrison
- **1911:** Edward Thomas Norris died (former Mayor of Arundel, brewery owner)
- **1914:** Widow Jessie Sarah Norris sold tied houses (The Globe, Chichester; Noah's Ark, Lurgashall) to Friary Brewery
- **1935:** Company liquidated
- **1935-1955:** Site used as Friary, Holroyd and Healy depot

The brewery owned or supplied many of the town's pubs during its operational life. Its closure consolidated the local pub market into the hands of regional and national pub companies.

**Source:** [Brewery History Society](https://breweryhistory.com/wiki/index.php/Eagle_Brewery,_Arundel)

## 7. Trends and Observations

### 7.1 Premiumisation and Food-Led Positioning
The majority of Arundel's surviving pubs have shifted toward food, accommodation, or both. Pure wet-led locals (Kings Arms, Eagle Inn) are the exception rather than the rule.

### 7.2 The Accommodation Play
At least 7 venues now offer rooms: Swan Hotel (14), White Swan (20), St Mary's Gate (6-7), Norfolk Arms (37), White Hart (rooms), Black Horse (rooms), Welldiggers Arms (14). This reflects Arundel's strong tourist trade driven by the Castle, Cathedral, and South Downs proximity.

### 7.3 Craft Brewing Renaissance
After the Eagle Brewery closed in 1935, Arundel had no local brewery for nearly 60 years. The founding of Arundel Brewery in 1992 and Fauna Brewing in 2020 has restored local production. Both operate taprooms, and their beers are stocked across the town's pubs.

### 7.4 The Norfolk Estate Influence
The Duke of Norfolk's estate remains a significant landlord. The Black Rabbit sits on estate land (operated by Hall & Woodhouse). Fauna Brewing occupies a former estate building (The Old Engine Shed). The Norfolk Arms Hotel was built by the 10th Duke. This aristocratic connection gives Arundel's pub scene a distinctive character compared to other Sussex market towns.

### 7.5 Community Ownership
The George at Burpham demonstrates a viable alternative model. Purchased by 16 local shareholders in 2006 to prevent closure, it has since achieved national acclaim (Top 50 Gastropubs, Good Food Guide Top 100). This model is increasingly discussed as a template for at-risk rural pubs.

### 7.6 Wet-Led Contraction
The Eagle Inn stopped serving food in late 2024, repositioning as drinks-only. This is counter to the prevailing trend and may reflect the difficulty of competing on food in a small town with several strong gastropub operators.
