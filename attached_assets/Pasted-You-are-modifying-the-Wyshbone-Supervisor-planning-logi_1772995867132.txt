You are modifying the Wyshbone Supervisor planning logic.

Goal
Implement a generic relationship-direction reasoning rule so that when a query asks about relationships between entities, the planner chooses the side most likely to publish evidence of that relationship.

Background
Currently the planner searches entities first and then tries to verify relationships on their websites. This often fails because the evidence usually exists on the other side of the relationship.

Example

User query:
Find organisations that work with the local authority in Blackpool

Current plan (weak):
SEARCH organisations → WEB_VISIT → try to prove relationship

Better plan:
Search the authority → extract partners → verify organisations

We want the planner to choose the best direction automatically.

---

Step 1 — Detect relationship queries

When extracted constraints include relationship predicates such as:

works_with
partner_of
funded_by
supported_by
supplier_to
commissioned_by
member_of
collaborates_with
contractor_to

Mark the query as:

relationship_query = true

---

Step 2 — Identify entities in the relationship

Example:

organisation → works_with → local authority

Left entity = organisation
Right entity = authority

---

Step 3 — Score which side is more likely to publish the relationship

Add a simple heuristic scoring function.

Authority / institutional entities get higher scores because they commonly publish partners.

Example scoring:

authority entities:
council
local authority
government
regulator
NHS
university
public body

score +3

large organisations or brands:

major retailer
large corporate
national charity
major brand

score +2

general businesses / small organisations:

score +1

---

Step 4 — Choose discovery direction

If score(right_entity) > score(left_entity)

Then discovery_direction = reverse

Meaning search starting from the right entity.

Example:

organisation works_with council

Planner should search council pages first.

---

Step 5 — Generate discovery queries

If direction = reverse:

Generate queries such as:

site:{authority_domain} partners
site:{authority_domain} "work with"
site:{authority_domain} organisations
site:{authority_domain} "in partnership with"
site:{authority_domain} community organisations

Also allow broader search:

"{authority_name} partners organisations"

---

Step 6 — Extract candidate organisations

From the search results:

extract organisation names
store as candidate_entities

---

Step 7 — Verify relationship

For each candidate:

WEB_VISIT
EVIDENCE_EXTRACT
Send evidence to Tower for judgement.

---

Step 8 — Logging

Emit a diagnostic artefact:

planner_relationship_direction

Example payload:

{
relationship_query: true,
left_entity: "organisation",
right_entity: "local authority",
chosen_direction: "authority_first",
reason: "authority more likely to publish partnerships"
}

This must appear in AFR for debugging.

---

Constraints

Do not modify:

execution loop
Tower judgement
UI behaviour

This change only affects planning logic in Supervisor.

---

Fallback

If both entities have similar scores or no clear authority entity exists:

Use the existing planning behaviour.

---

Goal

Enable Wyshbone to reason about where relationship evidence is most likely to exist, improving success rate for relationship queries across many domains.
