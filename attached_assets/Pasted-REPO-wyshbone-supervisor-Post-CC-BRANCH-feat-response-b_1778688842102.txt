CONTEXT
The response builder constructs a 4-bullet delivery message. It receives
both `verifiedCount` (the number of leads with per-lead match_valid=true)
and `towerVerdict` (the combined Tower's overall verdict — pass/fail/error).

When per-lead verification succeeds but the combined Tower call fails for
unrelated reasons (e.g. Tower service 502, combined-call constraint
plumbing not yet matching per-lead truth), the LLM-generated 4-bullet
reads `Tower verdict: fail` from the facts list and writes "No leads were
verified against your hard constraints" — directly contradicting the
verifiedCount of 1+ above it.

For example: "find micro pubs that opened in last 6 months in sussex"
- Per-lead Tower verify: 1 verified (Arundel Taphouse, with quote evidence)
- Combined Tower verdict: fail
- 4-bullet says "No leads were verified" — wrong

The verification line should reflect the COUNT (the truth), not the
combined Tower verdict (a separate signal that belongs to Market/Suggestion).

GOAL
Make the LLM-generated 4-bullet's Verification line always reflect
verifiedCount and deliveredCount, regardless of towerVerdict. Tower's
overall verdict can still influence Market/Suggestion phrasing.

NOTE
This fix narrows the response-builder to TRUST verifiedCount as the
source of truth for verification messaging. The towerVerdict is still
useful for Market/Suggestion phrasing (e.g. when Tower says fail and
scarcity Type B is set, "Market here looks genuinely thin" makes sense)
but it cannot OVERRIDE the per-lead verification count.

The underlying combined-Tower-verdict-says-fail-when-per-lead-passes bug
is separate — a follow-up investigation should find why skipCombinedTower
didn't fire for the micro pubs run. But fixing the response-builder
isolation here means a stale or wrong towerVerdict can never lie about
how many leads were actually verified.
